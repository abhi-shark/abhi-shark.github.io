Overview

This project involved designing and building an autonomous robot capable of sorting and delivering color-coded "pollen" to specific plants. The robot utilized finite state machine programming, sensors, and servo mechanisms to achieve the assigned task efficiently and reliably.

Background

As part of the ME588 Mechatronics course, the objective was to design a robot that autonomously sorts and delivers the correct color pollen to the designated plant while rejecting others. The robot's core strategy focused on targeting Plant 3, the closest plant to the starting barn. This simplified navigation and maximized efficiency, ensuring higher scores in the challenge.

Key Features

  • Chassis Design: A compact 3D-printed frame using PLA with three stacked layers for electronics, motors, and sorting mechanisms.
  • Motion System: Two DC motors with integrated encoders for precise movement.
  • Pollen Sorting: A TCS34725 color sensor coupled with servo motors for accurate sorting and delivery.
  • Power System: Dual power sources for motors (12V) and peripherals (5V) with a hardware kill switch for safety.
  • Finite State Machine (FSM): A Python-based control strategy for autonomous operation, including pre-sorting, gameplay, and shutdown phases.

Methodology

The robot was designed to prioritize efficiency and simplicity. It utilized motion planning to navigate a straight line between the barn and Plant 3, relying on encoders for precise distance measurement. A TCS34725 color sensor was calibrated to detect specific pollen colors, triggering servo motors to either store or reject the pollen. The finite state machine controlled the robot's operations, transitioning between pre-sorting, delivery, and reloading states based on sensor inputs and predefined conditions.

Results

The robot successfully completed all project requirements, including precise pollen sorting, autonomous navigation, and timed delivery. Despite setbacks, such as supply chain issues and hardware failures, the team adapted and achieved a functional system.

Learnings

Key takeaways included the importance of redundancy in navigation tools, the need for secure wiring to prevent hardware failures, and the value of simplifying design strategies to achieve robust functionality. Future iterations could incorporate advanced motion planning and IMU-based navigation for improved reliability.
